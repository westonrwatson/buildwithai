Build Lab 02

Your about me site.

Build a single-page About Me site in your AI chat, upload it to GitHub, and deploy something you'd put on a resume.

Before you build

Design principles.

What is bad

If it looks like it was built in 1998 and never updated — cramped text, clashing colors, no clear hierarchy — your visitors leave before they read a word.

Berkshire Hathaway

A wall of blue links. No hierarchy, no breathing room, no reason to stay.
Dreams of the Great Earth Changes

Clashing colors, drop shadows, and zero whitespace. Everything screams at once.
Craigslist

Dense columns of tiny links. Functional, but overwhelming and impossible to scan.

What works

Strong sites share the same basics: clear hierarchy, intentional color, generous space, and a focal point. Open these before you prompt your AI.

Boho Studio

Muted palette, elegant type, and room to breathe. Feels premium without trying too hard.

Odesa Metro

Bold contrast, one accent color, and a clear hero message. Personality with structure.

A24 Films

Cinematic imagery, minimal UI, and typography that leads your eye. Less is more.

Principles to apply

Use these six areas as a checklist when you build and iterate on your About Me site.

Color
Start with a palette
Use the 60-30-10 rule: 60% neutral background, 30% secondary, 10% accent. Generate palettes at Coolors or find color palette inspiration on Pinterest.
Type
Two fonts max
One for headings, one for body. Browse pairings at Google Fonts. Tell the AI: "Use [Font A] for headings and [Font B] for body text."
Components
Don't reinvent buttons
Ask the AI for polished buttons and cards — consistent padding, hover effects, and rounded corners. Need icons? Browse free SVGs at Heroicons.
Space
Whitespace is design
Cramped layouts feel amateur. Ask for "generous padding" and "consistent 8px spacing scale." When in doubt: "Make it feel more spacious and editorial."
Hierarchy
Guide the eye
The biggest, boldest element should be what matters most. Use size, weight, and color — not more colors — to show importance. One clear focal point per section.
Vibe
Describe the feeling
Say "minimal and clean like Apple," "bold and playful like Spotify," or "warm and personal like a letter." The AI understands aesthetic references.

Phase 01

Start from the template.

Download starter file Download prompt See Example
  1. Download the starter file and prompt — save the starter as index.html
  2. Rename 4 photos as photo1.jpg (hero), photo2.jpg, photo3.jpg, and photo4.jpg — keep them in the same folder as index.html
  3. Open Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini in your browser
  4. Fill in every field in the prompt document, then copy the prompt section at the bottom
  5. Upload index.html and all 4 photos to the chat, then paste your filled prompt
  6. Preview or download the updated index.html, save it over your file, and open it in your browser
  7. Check every item in the requirements list below
Your first vision will include
  • 4 sections — Home, About, Projects, and More on one page
  • Hero — your name, tagline, portrait photo, and 3 stats
  • About — a short bio, 3 interests, and a skills list
  • Projects — 3 project cards with photos
  • Values — 4 cards with facts, beliefs, or mottos
  • Navigation — fixed nav that scrolls to each section
  • Footer — your email, social links, and a contact message
Phase 02

Make it yours.

One change at a time. Paste or upload your current index.html only if the chat no longer has your latest version, then prompt a new change.

Make at least 1 adjustment.

Translate your site
"Translate my entire site into [YOUR LANGUAGE — e.g. Romanian, Russian, Ukrainian]. Keep the same layout and styling. Translate all headings, body text, button labels, and nav links."
Stats highlight
"Restyle my hero stat row — make the numbers bigger and bolder, add subtle dividers between stats, and color the numbers with my accent."
Interest icons
"Swap the icons in my 'Things I care about' list for SVGs from Heroicons that match each interest — [Interest 1], [Interest 2], and [Interest 3]."
Scroll animations
"Add subtle fade-in on scroll for each section and smooth hover effects on buttons, project cards, and value cards."
Values refresh
"Rename the 'More' nav link and section heading to [YOUR TITLE]. Rewrite the four value cards with punchier, more personal copy."
Client brief
"Create a fillable website brief I can send to [CLIENT NAME] before building their About Me site — include questions for their name, tagline, bio, 3 projects, 4 photos, color/style preferences, and social links, plus a prompt template at the bottom they can return to me."
Phase 03

Push & deploy.

Push to GitHub, connect to Vercel, and get a live URL you can share.

Push to GitHub
  1. Go to github.com and sign in.
  2. Click New (or the + icon) → New repository.
  3. Name it about-me and create the repo.
  4. On the empty repo page, click uploading an existing file.
  5. Upload your index.html, then click Commit changes.
Deploy on Vercel
  1. Go to vercel.com and sign in with GitHub.
  2. Click Add New → Project.
  3. Find about-me in your repos and click Import.
  4. Click Deploy — leave the default settings as they are.
  5. When the build finishes, copy your live URL (e.g. about-me.vercel.app).
