Talk to the AI

How to prompt.

Part 01
Role
Tell the AI who it is. Give it a persona that fits the task.
"You are an expert web developer."
Part 02
Task
Tell it exactly what to build or do. Be direct and specific.
"Build me a personal website."
Part 03
Context
Give it the details, constraints, and requirements it needs.
"Dark theme, single page. No frameworks."
Full example prompt — copy & fill in the blanks
You are an expert web developer. Build me a personal about-me website as a single index.html file. My name is [YOUR NAME]. I am [ONE SENTENCE ABOUT YOU]. Use a dark theme with clean, modern typography. Include a hero section with my name and tagline, a short about section, a skills section, and a footer with my email address. No frameworks. Vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript only. Make it something I'd actually want to show someone.
Get better results

Tips & tricks.

01
Be specific
Vague prompts get vague results. Describe the layout, colors, behavior, and constraints you want. The more detail you give, the closer the first output lands.
02
Give constraints
Tell it what you don't want. "No frameworks. Vanilla JS only." Constraints help the AI produce cleaner, more focused code that's easier to understand.
03
Iterate
Your first prompt is a draft. Ask it to change one thing at a time — "Make the hero section taller" or "Change the font to something more editorial." Small edits compound fast.
04
Ask why
Don't just copy the code. Ask: "Explain what this block does." You'll understand it faster than reading docs, and you'll know how to change it next time.
05
Paste the error
If something breaks, copy the error message and paste it directly into the chat: "I got this error — fix it." The AI can debug its own code faster than you can Google it.
06
Describe the vibe
You don't need to know technical terms. Say "Make it feel more editorial and clean" or "It should feel like a luxury brand." The AI understands aesthetic direction.
